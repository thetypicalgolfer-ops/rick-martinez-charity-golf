Picture it: blue skies, 144 golfers, flags flying, music going, and one electric morning where every swing means something. The 2nd Annual Rick Martinez Charity Golf Tournament isn't just a round of golf — it's the biggest, most patriotic, most heart-filled party San Antonio will throw all year. And it's all for the kids.

This is the day the whole community shows up in their boldest red, white & blue, foursomes talk a little trash on the first tee, and total strangers become lifelong friends by the back nine. There's laughter echoing across the fairways, cold drinks, and the kind of friendly competition that gets your heart pounding. You are going to want to be here.

One unforgettable day. Thousands in prizes. A lifetime of impact.

But here's the real reason we're out here…

Behind every contest, every prize, every laugh on the course, there is a child in San Antonio waiting for a chance they didn't choose to go without. A kid who goes to bed unsure about tomorrow. A child who is one mentor, one meal, one open door away from a completely different life.

That's who we golf for. Every dollar raised pours straight into the Learn for Life Center of San Antonio and the Loving Heart Foundation — education, mentorship, and real opportunity for kids who need someone in their corner. When you play, when you sponsor, when you give, you become that someone.

These children can't sponsor themselves.
They can't buy a ticket. They can't make the call.
But you can.

Maybe golf isn't your thing. Maybe July 10th doesn't work. Maybe you live a thousand miles away. We understand — life is busy. But please hear this gently: a child's need doesn't take a day off because your calendar is full. If you can't swing a club, you can still change a life. The cost of skipping out is one fewer kid who gets help this year — and that's a price they pay, not us.

So if you can play — play. If you can sponsor — sponsor. And if you truly can't be there… then this is your moment to give. Whatever you can. $10, $50, $500 — it all becomes a warm meal, a tutor, a safe place, a real future. Don't look away from a child who's counting on grown-ups to do the right thing. Be one of the good ones.
